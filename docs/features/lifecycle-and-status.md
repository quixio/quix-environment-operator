# Lifecycle & status

<!-- solution-docs:begin feature-lifecycle-and-status -->
## What it does

Every environment reports its lifecycle through the `Environment` status: an overall phase (`InProgress` → `Ready`, or `Failed`; `Deleting` during teardown), a human-readable message, per-resource statuses for the namespace and RoleBinding, and `observedGeneration` so callers can tell whether the status reflects their latest change. Significant actions and errors are additionally emitted as Kubernetes events, giving customers an audit trail with standard tooling.

## Why it exists

The platform drives provisioning programmatically and needs a machine-readable answer to "is this environment usable yet, and if not, why?". Customers and reviewers need the same story in `kubectl describe` form. Sub-resource statuses exist because "Failed" alone is not actionable — knowing *which* resource failed (and that a later reconcile recovered it) is.

## Constraints and guarantees

- **Finalizer-driven teardown.** Deletion is ordered: RoleBinding first, then the namespace, then waiting for the namespace to actually terminate before the `Environment` is released. Nothing is left behind, and nothing is released while cleanup is still pending.
- **Managed resources only.** Teardown verifies ownership labels; a namespace the operator didn't create for *this* environment is never deleted.
- **Self-healing.** Reconciliation is level-triggered and idempotent: drift in managed resources (deleted RoleBinding, stripped labels) is repaired on the next reconcile, and sub-resource statuses recover from `Failed` once the cause is gone.
